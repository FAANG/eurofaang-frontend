Aim & Objectives

BovReg will fill identify functional elements in the cattle genome and will establish a respective genome annotation catalogue. The improved annotation of the bovine genome will open new perspectives for understanding of the genetic architecture of complex traits in cattle. BovReg will provide knowledge, how the genetic and epigenetic make-up of cattle translates into divergent phenotypes and how it enables response to environmental challenges. Scientific and industrial breeding communities are in need of respective data to include predictive biology in new breeding and management tools for initiating innovative future cattle production systems.

Specific objectives are:
  • Establish new tools and resources for functional genome annotation
  • Annotate functionally active genomic regions in tissues that are that are highly relevant for the BovReg target traits:
    • health//disease susceptibility (mastitis)
    • robustness (fertility)
    • biological efficiency (nutrient conversion, greenhouse gas emission, feed efficiency, production) in dairy and beef cattle
  • Find and verify functionally relevant variation in annotated regulatory regions
  • Understand the epigenetic regulation, how environmental challenges impact the genome
  • Develop prototype models for integrating biological knowledge on regulatory variation in genomic selection
  • Provide targeted training, dissemination and communication activities
  • Assess social perception and ethical dimension of the use of novel genomic information

Concept

The BovReg concept comprises the consequential workflow and interaction of 11 WPs to establish a full knowledge hub that provides advances in basic sciences along with translational research and the ethical evaluation of the project’s potential applications.

BovReg will identify functionally active structural elements in the bovine genome and functionally validate candidate regulatory variants. The project will establish a comprehensive map of these elements based on cell lines and a comprehensive catalogue of tissues highly relevant to BovReg target traits. The tissue samples represent a wide spectrum of the bovine species (both gender, three divergent breeds representing different metabolic types and 5 ontogenetic stages) and are attached to comprehensive phenotypic metadata. Development and standardization of pipelines for the respective data analyses and deposit into public domain archives will be a sustainable legacy of our project, fully open to public access. Integrating data of whole-genome association studies for BovReg target traits with data on expression and metabolomic QTL and the effects of mobile genomic elements will further add to the map, and provide a resource for prioritising variants and identifying potential mechanisms underlying genetic associations. BovReg will use respective candidate regulatory variants for functional confirmation and for the development of more accurate genomic prediction tools for selective breeding of the target traits. A particular feature of BovReg is the analysis of ethical dimensions and societal perception of new genomic breeding technologies.
