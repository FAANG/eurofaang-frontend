GENE-SWitCH (the regulatory GENomE of SWine and CHicken: functional annotation during development) will deliver new underpinning knowledge on the functional genomes of two main monogastric farm species (pig and chicken) and enable its immediate translation to the pig and poultry sectors.

Why a focus on developmental stages?

The activation status of the functional (coding and regulatory) elements of the genome varies not only between cells and tissues but also over time. During the development in ovo and in utero, several functional elements orchestrate the temporal dynamics (“switches”) and tissue-specificity of genome activities, thus underpinning key steps in the determination of the phenotypes of the adult animal. These elements are often inactivated in adult tissues and therefore missed by the functional annotations of the genomes of farmed animal species. Filling this annotation gap is essential in studying the impact of the genetic and epigenetic variations of the genome on the phenotypes of relevance for sustainable animal productions.

Aims

A: Functional annotations across tissues and developmental stages

GENE-SWitCH will identify the functional elements acting in three different developmental stages (early organogenesis, late organogenesis and newborn/hatched) in a panel of seven tissues of pigs and chickens. The annotations will be integrated into the existing annotations in FAANG to deliver comprehensive genome annotation maps from embryo/foetus through adult life. Comparative genomic studies to identify patterns of conservation and variation will be carried out within this project (i.e. bird vs. mammal) and in collaboration with EuroFAANG partners.

B: Using functional annotation for precision animal breeding

The new reference annotation maps will provide excellent support to empirically investigate the potential benefit of the use of functional genome annotation for precision animal breeding: Genomic selection – to develop and validate innovative genomic prediction models that use functional annotation in combination with genotypic (SNPs, sequence variants) and phenotypic data, including intermediate phenotypes, to improve prediction accuracy of breeding values in commercial populations.

Diet X epigenetics - to assess if and how varying fibre content in the maternal diet (a major environmental factor) affects the epigenetic patterns of the pig foetus, and the possible persistence of epigenetic marks through the weaning stage.

C: Standardization of data and processes, dissemination and outreach

A wide set of dissemination, training and outreach activities will be developed to better inform European stakeholders on the benefits from an increased understanding of the value of genetic information including richly annotated high-quality genome sequences. The ultimate goal will be to fit stakeholders’ needs and expectations for improving the sustainability of the pig and poultry production systems.

Expected impact

  • High-quality, richly annotated reference annotation maps for pig and chicken available to the whole research community.
  • Cutting-edge research results paving the way to further studies and strategies in fundamental and applied animal sciences:
    • Adding up knowledge that associates the effects of genetic polymorphisms with fetal genome activity and their consequences for adult phenotypes.
    • Demonstration on how functional annotation of genomes can foster the advancement of genomic selection for chicken and swine productions.
    • Underpinning knowledge on the epigenetic effects of nutritional stimuli on the foetal genome and on their possibly persistence in adulthood to affect phenotypes.
  • European stakeholders benefit from an increased understanding of the value of functional genome annotation to face current and future challenges to achieve sustainable productions.

Project structure and partners

The project is structured in three pillars (1, 2, 3) and organised in eight WPs: WP1, 2 and 3 (light blue), WP4 and 5 (green), WP3 and 6 (dark blue), plus project coordination and ethical aspects (WP7, WP8).

GENE-SWitCH is a multi-actor project which brings together 11 partners representing European excellence, including partners who pioneered the worldwide FAANG initiative and two international leading companies in animal breeding and biotech industry.
