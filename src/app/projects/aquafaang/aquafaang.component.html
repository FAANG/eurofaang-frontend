Aim

Recent developments in genomics have advanced innovation in European aquaculture. However, despite creating reference-quality genome sequences for the main species, our understanding of genome functions remains limited. In close collaboration with end users, a new phase of research is needed to build on the recent advances in genomics for sustainable, competitive and profitable European aquaculture. Thus, the well-annotated reference genomes will have fundamental importance as a resource for the research community in both academia and industry. Moreover, the application of functional genomics is needed to improve genomic selection, including traits like disease resistance in farmed fish stocks to address the economic impact of infectious diseases and welfare problems.

The AQUA-FAANG (Advancing European Aquaculture by Genome Functional Annotation) project aims to greatly improve our understanding of genome function in the six most commercially important European farmed fishes (Figure 1) For each of the species, genome-wide annotation maps will be generated. This is a tool genome scientists use to link the genes and gene expression with the different traits of the fish. The annotation maps will be used to achieve a better understanding of how the genome contributes to variation in traits of commercial relevance. We will compare results across the six species and investigate if there are any similarities or transferability of findings.

An important aim is to improve the ability to predict traits using genomic information, paving the way for precision breeding to improve disease resistance and other commercial traits in aquaculture. The project combines world-leading interdisciplinary scientific expertise with industry, providing direct pathways to commercial exploitation.

One example is in the study of disease resistance (Figure 2), where the fish’s susceptibility can be explored by performing a challenge test. Fish are exposed to viruses or bacteria, and this may lead to a change in the fish’s genomic expression in order to survive the attack. The AQUA-FAANG genome researchers are investigating this effect to be able to pinpoint the genes or gene expressions that are crucial for surviving such an attack. This information can be very useful in breeding programs to select the more susceptible fish for breeding the next generations.

Objective

The AQUA-FAANG project will contribute to advancing European aquaculture in several ways. An overview of the objectives is given in Figure 3.

Impact

AQUA-FAANG created an internationally leading consortium for excellence and innovation in fish genomics. The cluster of world experts from both academia and industry, combined with extensive training and dissemination activities, will create a long-term interactive hub expected to outlast the duration of the project. Project impacts can be summarized as:

  • AQUA-FAANG will deliver comprehensive high-quality genome functional annotation maps for the six most economically important fish species in European aquaculture, providing insight into variation across tissues, life stages and experimental conditions using a range of core FAANG functional annotation assays.
  • Pave the way for subsequent use of annotated genomes to improve precision breeding in farmed animal production, by linking genome to phenotype and improving means to measure/record phenotypes.
  • The project will test and optimize approaches for prioritizing functional variations in QTL regions across several species and harness those variants to improve genomic selection.
  • The project will deliver a computational pipeline that academic and commercial researchers can use to prioritize functional variants in candidate regions of the genome (e.g. QTL/GWAS regions) which will facilitate direct exploitation of functional annotation datasets for marker assisted and genomic selection application.
  • The project will develop novel approaches to characterize and measure immune cell heterogeneity in fishes defining immune cellular phenotypes at an unprecedented level of accuracy, which can be used in the future to improve precision breeding by enhancing the information-content in recorded phenotypes in terms of host defence mechanisms responsible for disease resistance.
  • Progress in understanding genotype per environment interactions and deciphering the mechanisms by which some effects induced by environment/stressors can be transmitted across generations.
  • AQUA-FAANG will contribute strongly to international coordinated actions aiming to achieve standardized genome functional annotation in domesticated animals and fishes.

In the long-term, activities will enhance the sustainability of farmed animal production

  • A key long-term impact of AQUA-FAANG will be the project’s contribution towards improving traits of the farmed fish, such as enhanced disease resistance, in each of the main farmed fish species in Europe.
  • The data and analyses produced by the project will enable and enhance follow-on research in a number of key areas of relevance to sustainable European aquaculture.

Project structure and partners

The AQUA-FAANG project brings together world-leading interdisciplinary scientific expertise, with partners located in eight European countries. In total, there are 24 partners in the project: 15 academic partners and 10 representing the industry.

The project is structured into 9 work packages (WPs). The first six WPs are research driven and integrate their research across all six fish species.
